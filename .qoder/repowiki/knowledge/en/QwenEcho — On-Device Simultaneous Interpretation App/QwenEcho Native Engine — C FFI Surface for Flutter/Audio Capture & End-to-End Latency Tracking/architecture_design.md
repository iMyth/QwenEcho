Three cooperating C++ components sharing a common `native_port.h` bridge:

- `audio_ring_buffer.h` — header-only SPSC lock-free circular buffer over `int16_t` PCM samples. Uses power-of-two capacity with bitmask indexing, `std::atomic<uint32_t>` head/tail with acquire/release ordering, and 64-byte cache-line separation (`alignas(64)`) between `write_pos_` and `read_pos_` to avoid false sharing. Overflow policy is overwrite-oldest (producer advances read pointer via `advance_read_on_overflow`).
- `audio_collector.cpp` — wraps platform HAL (`hal_audio_capture_*`, `hal_thread_set_realtime_priority`) into an opaque `AudioCollector` handle. The RT audio callback writes directly into the shared `AudioRingBuffer` without allocation or blocking; drop detection compares `expected_samples` (derived from `steady_clock` elapsed time at 16 kHz mono) against `actual_samples` and posts `MSG_SAMPLE_DROP` through `native_port_post_sample_drop` when gap exceeds 10 ms.
- `latency_tracker.cpp` — mutex-guarded fixed-size array of `LatencyRecord`s tracking one in-flight segment per `segment_id`. Each stage exposes an `on_*` hook (`on_segment_lock`, `on_asr_start/end`, `on_llm_start/first_token`, `on_tts_start/first_audio`) that timestamps the record and checks stage budgets (`LATENCY_BUDGET_ASR_MS`, `LATENCY_BUDGET_LLM_MS`, `LATENCY_BUDGET_TTS_MS`) plus E2E budget (`LATENCY_BUDGET_E2E_NORMAL_MS` / `_THROTTLE_MS`), posting `MSG_LATENCY_WARNING` via `native_port_post_latency_warning` on violation. Thermal throttle mode switches the E2E budget between 800 ms and 1200 ms.

Dependency direction: both collectors depend only on `audio_ring_buffer.h` + `native_port.h`; they do not depend on each other. The ring buffer has no dependencies beyond the standard library.
